Captain Vikram Batra

(9 september 1974 - 7 July 1999)

About Vikram Batra

  • Vikram Batra was born on 9 September 1974, in a small town in Palampur, Himachal Pradesh.
  • He passed the CDS (Combined Defence Services) examination in 1996.
  • Commissioned into the 13 JAK Rif of the Indian Army in December 1997.
  • He is also known as ‘Sher Shah’ because of his fearless leadership in the Kargil War
  • His bravery was most noticed in the 1999 Kargil War between India and Pakistan.
  • During the war, he led his team to victory on the difficult battlefield of Point 5140.
  • He made the famous statement, “Yeh dil maange more,” which became a symbol of India’s victory.
  • Demonstrated exceptional courage and leadership under heavy fire.
  • Unfortunately, he was martyred on July 7, 1999, while rescuing an injured officer during combat.
  • For his bravery, the Indian government honored him with the Param Vir Chakra, India’s highest military honor.
  • Inspirational figure whose spirit lives on in the hearts of Indians.
  • Vikram Batra is a hero and a role model for every Indian and his story inspires us all.
